Global Warming Overview

Global warming is the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the release of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing a gradual rise in temperature with significant environmental consequences.

Key points about global warming include:

  • Driven by fossil fuel burning, deforestation, and industrial processes.
  • Consequences: heatwaves, ice cap melting, rising sea levels, extreme weather, and ecosystem disruptions.
  • Threats to biodiversity, food security, and human health.
  • Mitigation involves reducing emissions, transitioning to renewables, and adopting sustainable practices.
  • Adaptation measures are essential to cope with ongoing changes.

International efforts like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global temperature rise.

Causes of Global Warming

Primary causes are fossil fuel burning, deforestation, industrial processes, agriculture, and waste management.

Effects of Global Warming

Effects include rising temperatures, heatwaves, ice cap melting, sea level rise, intense storms, and threats to biodiversity.
